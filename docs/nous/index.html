FAQ

What is operational intelligence?

The practice of building a structured model of a business from its existing documents, processes, and decisions. Unlike project management (tracks tasks) or BI (visualizes metrics), operational intelligence perceives the full operational state. Gartner calls the broader category Digital Twin of an Organization (DTO) and projects 25% enterprise adoption by 2026.

Does my data leave my network?

No. All processing runs on your hardware. No cloud. No external API calls with your data. You own everything. We access your systems via SSH during the engagement and delete credentials when complete.

What infrastructure do I need?

Python 3.11+ and SQLite (included with Python). 8GB RAM. No Docker, no cloud services, no external databases. Ollama is optional for local AI model training. If you have a laptop, you can run Nous.

How is this different from Monday, Notion, or Asana?

Those tools track tasks. Nous perceives your entire operational environment and builds a structured model that scores everything, detects what is missing, and maps dependencies. It is the intelligence layer beneath task trackers. Monday.com's enterprise contracts run $96K-$140K/year for project visibility alone. Nous provides operational intelligence for less.

How is this different from Celonis or process mining?

Process mining requires structured event log data from ERP systems and costs $200K-$1M+/year. Nous starts from your actual documents. Most businesses do not have clean event logs. They have contracts, SOPs, project files, and records. Nous starts where your data actually lives.

What AI models does it use?

You choose. Classification and scoring are deterministic -- no AI model, no hallucination, no cost. For document generation and analysis, you can use local models (trained on your data, running on your hardware) or external APIs (Claude, GPT) or both. The system scores all outputs and suggests the best. You select.

What if the system finds problems?

That is the point. The system honestly surfaces stale processes, blocked work, unprotected IP, revenue gaps, and compliance holes. A system that reveals its own gaps is more valuable than one that hides them. Gaps are the deliverable.

Do I keep everything?

Yes. The database, all reports, the trained model, the full CLI toolset. You can run Nous independently after the engagement ends. This is not SaaS. There is no lock-in. The value is in the intelligence, not the access.
